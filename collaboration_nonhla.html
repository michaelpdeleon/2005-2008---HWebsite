“Non-HLA genes in the HLA complex in HLA and Disease Associations”

The GENOMIC IMMUNOEPIDEMIOLOGY Laboratory will be primarily investigating the relevance of non-HLA gene content of the HLA complex in observed HLA and disease associations, the contribution of iron-related genes to cancer susceptibility and the genetic basis of gender effect in prenatal selection, childhood cancer/infection and aging.

We will need three types of DNA collections for large, sufficiently powered genetic epidemiology studies with replication:

1) Any well-characterized case-control and/or family-trio samples from cancers (especially childhood leukemias) that
a) have previously shown HLA associations (to assess the role of non-HLA genes of the HLA complex)
b) have high male-to-female ratios (in particular childhood lymphoma/leukemia and Kaposi sarcoma)
c) are related to iron excess (particularly leukemias, breast cancer and gastro-intestinal cancers)

We are particularly interested in cancers developed in subjects with inflammatory bowel diseases and secondary cancers following radiotherapy for a primary cancer.

2) Collections of DNA samples from newborns (with sex information) and couples with a history of recurrent miscarriages of unknown etiology.

3) Collections of DNA samples from well-characterized aging cohorts, in particular from centenarians with matching samples from the younger subjects of the same ancestry and geographic location.

We are also interested in large sets of fully HLA-typed population samples, preferably complete families, for HLA haplotyping studies including non-HLA gene variants.

To achieve our aims in genetic immunology and immunoepidemiology work, we need partnerships. This can be simply provision of samples but we prefer collaborative testing of specific hypotheses and/or direct laboratory-based collaboration. Also, we have opportunities for highly motivated and qualified researchers to work in our laboratories. We look forward to hearing from prospective partners.
