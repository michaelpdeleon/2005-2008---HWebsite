“Interferon-λ1 and Asthma”

Background: Asthma is a chronic, inflammatory disorder of the lungs and airway. It causes a significant, and increasing, degree of morbidity and mortality in individuals of all ages and represents an expanding healthcare problem. In asthma, elements of the immune system cause hyperresponsive airway constriction to a variety of allergic, environmental and physiological triggers. Recently, it has been discovered that one cytokine in particular, interleukin-13 (IL-13) is key to driving the asthmatic hyperreactivity. Much research is now being focused on novel ways of regulating or antagonizing IL-13 as a means of controlling asthma and providing patient relief.

Recent discoveries: A novel cytokine, Interferon-λ1, was recently discovered. This cytokine has anti-viral properties reminiscent of Type-I interferons, but we have discovered that it has unsuspected, profound, immunoregulatory properties, too. Of special interest is its capability to downregulate the expression of IL-13. Thus, Interferon-λ1 has excellent potential to be a novel therapeutic anti-asthmatic. Our interest in this possibility is heightened by a recent observation in asthma patients. Volunteer patients make 1/3 to 1/2 the levels of Interferon-λ1 when exposed to respiratory viruses, relative to healthy controls. Thus, in asthma patients, there is a significant deficiency of this important regulator of the main driver of the asthmatic response, IL-13 that emphasizes the importance of Interferon-λ1.

Opportunities for collaboration: We cannot realize the significance of our work without a critical scientific and clinical contribution from partnering physicians and other enthusiastic researchers who work in asthma. We would like to partner in two areas. First, with individuals who will provide us with clinical material of excellent diagnostic and treatment history. We cannot succeed without such partnerships. Second, we would like to open our laboratory to motivated individuals from within clinical and laboratory research to actively participate in our bench research. Such collaborations will connect basic researchers with clinical thinking in a hybrid academic / commercial environment.
